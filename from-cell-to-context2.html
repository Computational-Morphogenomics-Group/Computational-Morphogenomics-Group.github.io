How do individual cells maintain and interpret their identity in context? This theme centers the spatial and temporal cues that inform cell fate: trajectory inference, spatial omics, and developmental mapping, including how these principles vary across species.

Patches: Decoding Shared and Condition-Specific Programs in Wound Healing (2024)

Patches is a representation learning framework that captures both shared and condition-specific transcriptional programs during wound healing. It uses deep generative modeling to analyze spatial transcriptomic datasets and uncover the latent structures that govern tissue regeneration in different contexts.

A Computational Pipeline for Spatial Mechano-Transcriptomics (2023)

This pipeline integrates mechanical modeling with spatial gene expression data to study how physical forces shape gene expression patterns. It enables researchers to map mechano-sensitive gene expression domains and uncover the coupling between force and fate in developing tissues.

MarkerMap: Nonlinear Marker Selection for Single-Cell Studies (2024)

MarkerMap introduces a nonlinear approach to marker gene selection in single-cell studies, enabling more accurate and biologically meaningful classification across complex datasets. By capturing gene-gene interactions and nonlinear effects, MarkerMap enhances the ability to distinguish between subtle cell states in developmental and regenerative systems.

  • GitHub

In Silico Tissue Generation and Power Analysis for Spatial Omics (2023)

This work presents a simulation framework for generating synthetic spatial omics data with tunable structure and noise. It enables benchmarking and statistical power analysis for spatial transcriptomic studies, helping researchers design robust experiments before collecting data.
